The Conversations
Three Challenges. Real Answers.
Every Spark roundtable is built around three operational challenges that keep coming up in our conversations with CXOs across industries. These aren't product demos — they're peer discussions anchored in what organizations like yours are actually doing today.

Automating Complex Operations

Your best people are trapped in repetitive, multi-system workflows — order processing, supplier coordination, field dispatch. Hear how peers are deploying autonomous digital workers that handle these workflows end-to-end, giving 90,000+ hours back to field teams and returning $3M/year at scale. We'll discuss what's working, what isn't, and where the line between automation and human judgment really is.

From Concept to Production — Fast

You've run the pilots. Maybe they worked, maybe they didn't. Either way, the gap between "AI proof of concept" and "AI in production at 3 AM when the power goes out" is where most organizations stall. Hear how peers are co-building custom AI solutions — built with frontline workers, not for them — and deploying in weeks, not years. A global distillery went from 38% emergency repairs to predictive maintenance, projecting savings in the millions annually. A utility restored power 40% faster in its first deployment.

The Renaissance Framework

Inspired by Zack Kass's The Next Renaissance — AI isn't replacing human potential, it's expanding it. The question isn't what AI can do, but what we allow it to do. Every Spark conversation centers on the same idea: how Industrial AI frees people from invisible work so they can do what only humans can.

Why This Format Works

Three slides keeps it honest. No vendor names required. No corporate polish expected. This format is what generates the richest peer discussion — because it gives everyone in the room a shared framework to compare notes and challenge assumptions. The best Spark conversations happen when someone says "we tried that too, and here's what actually happened."
